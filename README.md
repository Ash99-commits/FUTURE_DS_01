## üßæ Project Overview

The  Dashboard provides a comprehensive view of business performance across sales, profit, product categories, customers, and regions. ## üîçKey Insights

- **Overall Performance:-**  
The store achieved $2.30M in total sales, $0.29M in net profit, and sold 37.87K units across 793 customers. Operations spanned 49 states, 531 cities, and 1,862 products.

- **Growth Trend (2011‚Äì2014):-**  
Sales rose from $0.16M to $0.48M, a 200% increase, while profits surged from $15K to $55K, up 266%, indicating strong year-over-year growth.

- **Regional Performance:-**  
The West ($0.73M) and East ($0.68M) regions together contributed 61% of total sales and nearly 70% of total profit (West: $0.10M, East: $0.09M).  
The Central ($0.50M) and South ($0.39M) regions delivered moderate results, signalling potential growth opportunities.  
While the Central region records higher sales than the South, its profit ($0.04M) remains lower, indicating inefficiencies or higher operating costs in that region.  

- **State-Level Highlights:-**  
California ($0.46M) and New York ($0.31M) jointly contributed ~34% of total sales, leading all states.

- **Category & Segment Insights:-**  
Technology led category sales with $0.83M and profits of $0.14M, outperforming Furniture and Office Supplies.  
The Consumer segment dominated with $1.16M in sales (51%) and $0.13M in profit (‚âà45%), reaffirming it as the core customer base.

- **Sub-Category Analysis:-**  
Top 5 Sub-Categories (Phones, Chairs, Storage, Tables, Binders) accounted for 56% of total sales; Phones ($0.33M) and Chairs ($0.32M) led the chart.  
Top 5 Profit Contributors (Copiers, Phones, Accessories, Paper, Binders) generated 72% of total profit, with Copiers alone earning $55.62K.  
Loss-Making Sub-Categories: Tables (-$17.7K), Bookcases (-$3.4K), and Supplies (-$1.19K) recorded the largest losses, followed by Fasteners and Machines.  
Lowest-Selling Sub-Categories: Fasteners, Labels, Envelopes, Art, and Supplies.

- **Product Performance:-**  
The Canon imageCLASS 2200 Advanced Copier was the best-performing product with $62K in sales and $25.20K in profit, outperforming its nearest competitor by nearly $30K in sales and $18K in profit.  
The Cubify CubeX 3D Printer Double Head Print recorded the highest loss of $8.88K.

- **Customer Insights:-**  
Jonathan Doherty placed the most orders (150), while Sean Miller topped sales contribution with $25.04K.  
The Consumer segment comprised 51.94% of customers, reaffirming its dominance.  

- **Seasonality:-**  
Peak months for both sales and profit were September ($0.31M, $37K), November ($0.35M, $36K), and December ($0.33M, $43K) ‚Äî likely due to festive and year-end buying cycles.

- **Distribution & Shipping:-**  
Office Supplies made up 60.30% of product distribution, surpassing Technology and Furniture.  
Standard Class was the dominant shipping mode, used in 59.72% of deliveries.  
The West region had the largest customer share (32.05%), followed by the East (28.50%).
